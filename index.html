About Me

Ahmed Ameen Fateh is a researcher in biomedical engineering whose work focuses on neuroimaging, resting-state fMRI, machine learning, dynamic functional connectivity, and computational psychiatry. His research aims to identify biologically meaningful brain connectivity markers for psychiatric and neurological disorders and to develop data-driven models for diagnosis and prediction.

He received his B.Sc. degree in Biomedical Engineering from the Lebanese International University, Yemen, and his M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China. His academic interests include translational neuroimaging research, machine learning in mental health, and brain-network analysis across developmental and clinical populations.
